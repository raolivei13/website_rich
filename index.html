Richard Oliveira

Hello! My name is Richard, and I'm a Marie Sk≈Çodowska-Curie Doctoral fellow and Phd candidate at Politecnico di Milano, Italy, in the department of Electronics, Information Technology, and Bioengineering (DEIB) and the department of Mathematics (DMAT), under the supervision of Dr. Stefano Savazzi . Furthermore, I am also affiliated with the Institute of Electronics, Computer and Telecommunication Engineering (IEIIT) at the Consiglio Nazionale delle Ricerche .

My background can be broadly defined as that of a Mathematical Engineer with a focus on developing mathematical models for how information is acquired, communicated and processed in engineered systems, with applications in control, computation and Machine Learning. My research centers on foundational advances in networked dynamical systems, spanning social networks, sensor-actuator networks, socio-technical systems, and ecosystems, and the development of distributed optimization and control algorithms that balance economic, engineering, social, and environmental objectives. In particular, my doctoral work will pioneer Bayesian Federated Learning algorithms for localization and mapping, designing and analyzing incentive-aware, game-theoretic approaches to client participation in both centralized and de-centralized network settings, bridging the gap between frequentist and Bayesian frameworks to steer complex networks toward desired outcomes.

I received my MSc. in Electrical Engineering, Signal and Image Processing and a BSc. in Electrical Engineering, Machine Learning and Controls from the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department at the University of California, San Diego, La Jolla, USA. During my MSc. studies I have worked on the theory of Discrete Pearson-Rayleigh Random Walks under the supervision of Prof. Massimo Franceschetti.

My additional research interests include inverse problems and mathematical signal processing.
