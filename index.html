Richard Oliveira

Hello! My name is Richard, and I am a Junior Data Scientist working on Large Language Models (LLMs) for a startup.

I received my MSc. in Electrical Engineering, Signal and Image Processing and a BSc. in Electrical Engineering, Machine Learning and Controls from the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department at the University of California, San Diego, La Jolla, USA. During my MSc. studies I have worked on the theory of Discrete Pearson-Rayleigh Random Walks under the supervision of Prof. Massimo Franceschetti.

My background can be broadly defined as that of a Mathematical Engineer. My research interests are on the mathematics of Data Science, at the intersection of Functional Analysis, Signal Processing, Machine Learning, Nonparametric Statistics, and Optimization. I am interested in investigating the mathematical foundations of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs), exploring critical questions about Regularization, Neural Network (NN) function learning, with potential applications in the field of Control Theory and Robotics.

My additional research interests include Inverse Problems, Geometry of Banach Spaces, Bayesian and Discrete Optimization, and Model Predictive Control (MPC).

Current Works

Currently, I am independently writing a paper on characterizing the solution of variational Inverse Problems as superpositions of shifted Heaviside functions scaled by appropriate weights.
